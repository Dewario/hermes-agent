## 2. Civil discovery motion procedures

### Preemption note (critical)

**Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.20** preempts local court rules relating to pleadings, demurrers, ex parte applications, motions, **discovery**, provisional remedies, and form/format of papers, except as permitted by statute or CRC. San Bernardino therefore does **not** maintain a freestanding local “discovery code.” Discovery substance and many motion requirements come from the **CCP** and **CRC**; local rules mainly regulate calendaring, filing location, meet-and-confer enforcement for calendar control, and tentative rulings.

### Meet and confer

| Source | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| CCP discovery chapters (e.g., §§ 2016.040, 2030.300, 2031.310, 2033.290, etc.) | Statutory meet-and-confer before many discovery motions |
| **Local R. 560** | Court may take off calendar motions **not supported by a declaration** establishing that an in-person or telephonic meet-and-confer (or other required meet-and-confer) occurred, or if the declaration is deficient |
| Complex Litigation Guidelines (not Local Rules) | Extra meet-and-confer before hearing; notice of conference results; **IDC required before discovery motion** in complex program (see §9) |
| Department Discovery Conference / IDC Orders | Often require meet-and-confer to impasse before IDC; failure may vacate IDC / invoke Local R. 560 |

**Confirmed local rule number:** San Bernardino Sup. Ct. Local R. **560**.

### Filing format / page limits

| Topic | Governing source | Local rule? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Motion format, captions, exhibits, bookmarks | CRC 2.100 et seq., 3.1110, 3.1112, 3.1113; eFiling requirements page | No separate local page-limit rule found for civil discovery motions |
| Memorandum page limits | **CRC 3.1113** (standard statewide limits; e.g., opening memo generally 15 pages unless court permits longer) | No San Bernardino local override located in July 1, 2026 rules |
| Subsequent papers | **Local R. 510** — file in the department where the matter is scheduled, within time prescribed by statute/CRC | Yes — Local R. 510 |
| Exhibits lodging | **Local R. 571** — exhibits generally filed (not lodged) unless lodging authorized | Yes — Local R. 571 |
| Tentative rulings | **Local R. 561** — optional tentative rulings; website after 3:00 p.m. court day before hearing; CRC 3.1308(a)(2) appearance practice | Yes — Local R. 561 (amended through July 1, 2026) |

**Do not invent a local page-limit rule number.** Use CRC 3.1113 unless a department order or case-management order states otherwise.

### Scheduling / calendaring

| Source | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| **Local R. 520** | No motion noticed without first **reserving** a hearing date with the clerk. Except CCP § 128.7 motions, UD summary proceedings under CCP § 1159 et seq., and summary judgment motions, **moving papers must be filed within five court days** of reserving the date |
| **Local R. 521** | Filing fees (except § 128.7 and MSJ) paid within **five days** of reserving; separate fee per combined motion |
| **Local R. 550** | Continuance / non-proceed notice to JA and opposing counsel by **4:30 p.m. of the second court day** before hearing |
| Court Civil Motion Reservation page | Email reservation (SBJC / Rancho / Victorville boxes); dates Mon–Thu; ≥3 weeks out; ≥3 proposed dates; file/pay within 5 court days of confirmation or reservation auto-cancels per Local R. 520 |
| Civil eFiling Requirements | General civil motion (incl. ex parte) **must be reserved before eFiling** or will be rejected (UD/LLT exception) |

**Confirmed local rule numbers:** Local R. **520**, **521**, **550**, **561**.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false` for Local R. 510/520/521/550/560/561 as stated in the July 1, 2026 PDF. `true` for any department-specific briefing overlays on top of those rules.

## 3. Discovery cutoff rules

**Local standing discovery cutoff order?**
**Not found** in the July 1, 2026 Local Rules as a countywide civil standing cutoff.

**How cutoff is set in practice:**
1. **Default statewide statute:** CCP §§ **2024.020** et seq. (completion of discovery generally on or before the **30th day before the date initially set for trial**; discovery motions earlier — typically 15 days before that initial trial date under § 2024.020(a) for discovery completion / related motion timing — confirm exact statutory subsections before relying).
2. **Case-specific court orders:** Trial Setting Conference minute orders, department Trial Setting Orders, and complex Case Management Orders may set or adjust discovery completion dates.
3. **Department trial orders:** Multiple departments’ standing trial orders state that timely propounded discovery not timely responded to before the “discovery cut-off” will be excluded at trial (e.g., Dept. S26 courtroom rules dated Jan. 20, 2026) — they **reference** a cutoff; they do not appear to create a countywide substitute for CCP § 2024.020.
4. **Complex cases:** Complex Litigation Guidelines impose an **automatic stay of discovery** pending the Initial Case Management Conference (or further order / party agreement) — this is a program guideline tied to Local R. **412**, not a general civil standing cutoff.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `true` for the operative cutoff in any live matter (read the TSC / CMO / trial-setting order). `false` for the finding that there is no countywide Local Rule creating a standing discovery cutoff independent of CCP / case orders.

## 4. Expert witness disclosure cutoff

**Local civil expert-disclosure rule:** **Not found** in the July 1, 2026 Local Rules for general civil practice.

**What governs instead:**
- **CCP §§ 2034.210–2034.310** (demand for exchange of expert trial witness information; timing keyed to the initial trial date; expert discovery completion rules in § 2034.030 / related provisions — confirm current statutory text).
- Expert-related **Local Rules Chapter 14 (Rules 1419 et seq.)** concern **court-appointed criminal / ancillary experts and fees**, not civil party expert disclosure under CCP § 2034.
- Department Discovery Conference Orders may **carve out expert discovery disputes** from IDC requirements (example: S33 Order re Discovery Conference, revised Dec. 29, 2025 / 2026 version — expressly does **not** apply to expert discovery disputes).

**Procedure summary for plaintiff-side practice (state law, not local rule):**
1. Serve demand for exchange of expert information under CCP § 2034.210 et seq. (timing relative to initial trial date).
2. Exchange expert lists / declarations as required.
3. Complete expert discovery per CCP timing unless court order or stipulation alters it.
4. Check assigned department trial-setting / CMO for any earlier expert exchange date.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `true` — no local substitute rule confirmed; confirm case orders and current CCP text.

## 5. Mandatory settlement conference / trial setting (discovery timing impacts)

| Rule | Substance (July 1, 2026 Local Rules) |
| --- | --- |
| **Local R. 410** | MSC **may be ordered** on dates set at case management or trial setting conference. Exemption language for general civil cases filed before Jan. 1, 2020 |
| **Local R. 411.1** | **Initial Trial Setting Conference** in unlimited and limited civil cases **in lieu of** a CMC; clerk sets date at filing, on or before the **26th week** from filing; Initial TSC Statement (local form #13-09001-360) due **10 days** before; non-appearance review unless appearance required; further TSCs may require At Issue Memorandum **15 days** prior |
| **Local R. 411.2** | **Trial Readiness Conference** in unlimited civil and complex cases; trial docs / MIL timing; MILs comply with Local R. 415 |
| **Local R. 412** | Complex cases governed by Complex Litigation Program Guidelines (posted on court site) |
| **Local R. 601** | Adopts **CRC 3.1380** for mediations / MSCs |
| **Local R. 611** | Trial counsel, parties, and persons with full settlement authority must attend MSC unless excused |
| **Local R. 409** | ADR review at CMC/TSC |

**Discovery-timing effects (practice, not a single local cutoff rule):**
- Discovery windows usually run against the **initial trial date** (CCP), which the court sets through the **TSC / further setting** process under Local R. 411.1.
- Some department MSC / trial orders and court MSC-week notices have required discovery to be complete before MSC participation — treat as **event-specific**, not a countywide Local Rule.
- Complex cases: discovery stayed until Initial CMC under Complex Guidelines; subsequent CMOs drive phased discovery.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false` for Local R. 410 / 411.1 / 411.2 / 601 / 611 text; `true` for whether a particular MSC order requires completed discovery.

## 6. Department assignments for civil discovery motions

**Confirmed local rule:** **Local R. 403** — cases under Chapter 4 receive an **all-purpose judge assignment at filing**. The all-purpose judge handles the case, including discovery motions.

**There is no separate countywide “Discovery Department” identified in the Local Rules** for general civil motions to compel.

**Practical calendaring:**
- Motions (including motions to compel) are reserved to the **assigned civil department** (San Bernardino Justice Center S-series; Rancho Cucamonga R-series; Victorville V-series, etc.).
- **Local R. 561(f)** lists civil courtroom phone groupings used for tentative-ruling inquiries (departments such as S14, S16, S17, S22–S33, S35–S37, R12, R14, R17, V11 — list as amended July 1, 2026). Treat the Schedule of Assignments as authoritative for current judge/department pairing.
- Civil Division calendaring guidelines list **IDC** and **Motions** as hearing types with days/times varying by courtroom.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false` for all-purpose assignment structure (Local R. 403); `true` for the current judge/department and that department’s IDC order.

## 9. Informal Discovery Conference (IDC)

### Countywide Local Rule?

**No.** The July 1, 2026 Local Rules **Chapter 5** does not contain a countywide IDC-before-compel rule analogous to some other California counties’ local rules.

### Where IDC *does* apply

| Source | Status | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Civil Division Calendaring Guidelines | Current practice document | Lists **Informal Discovery Conference (IDC)** as a calendared hearing type; days/times vary by department |
| Complex Litigation Guidelines (Local R. 412 points here) | Program guidelines | After statutory meet-and-confer fails, moving party **must request informal conference before filing any discovery motion**; request **automatically stays** the motion-filing deadline; remote; briefing not required under the guidelines text |
| Department “Discovery Conference Order” / “IDC Order” | Judge-specific; many civil depts publish one | Often **mandatory** before hearing (or before filing) discovery motions; joint statement page limits and timing vary |
| Example — Dept. **S26** (Hon. Kevin Lee), Discovery Conference Order (Jan. 20, 2026) | Department order | IDC **required before filing** any discovery motion; Zoom; Notice of Hearing + briefs ≥7 court days prior; joint brief ≤5 pages / individual ≤3 pages |
| Example — Dept. **S26** Courtroom Rules (Jan. 20, 2026) | Department order | IDC required before court hears discovery motions; scheduling IDC stays deadline in **complex** matters; unlimited civil parties encouraged to stipulate to stay; joint IDC statement page/timing requirements stated in courtroom rules (cross-check against that dept’s Discovery Conference Order — wording has varied between “3” and “7” court days / page limits across documents) |
| Example — Dept. **S33** Order re Discovery Conference (revised Dec. 29, 2025; “2026 version”) | Department order | Within 5 court days of filing discovery motion (and before opposition), schedule video IDC; carve-outs for self-represented disputes, **expert discovery**, and **motions to compel where no responses at all**; joint IDC statement ≤5 pages filed/lodged in S33 1 week prior; Local R. 560 meet-and-confer enforcement |

**Courtroom Documents index** (court PDF) shows Discovery Conference / IDC materials for many departments (e.g., S17, S22, S25, S26, S29, S30, S31, S33, R17, etc.). **Always pull the assigned department’s current PDF.**

### Bottom line for plaintiff-side discovery motions

- San Bernardino is **not** “IDC-free,” but IDC is **department- and program-driven**, not a single Local Rule number like “Local R. XXX – IDC.”
- For **complex** cases, treat IDC as effectively mandatory under Complex Guidelines.
- For **unlimited/limited** general civil, assume IDC if the assigned department publishes a Discovery Conference / IDC Order — many do.
- Confirm whether IDC must occur **before filing** (S26-style) or **after filing / before opposition** (S33-style) — **they differ.**

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `true` for the assigned department’s current IDC order; `false` for the finding that no countywide Local Rule mandates IDC.

## Quick plaintiff-side checklist (San Bernardino civil)

1. Confirm assigned **all-purpose department** (Local R. 403) and pull that department’s **Discovery Conference / IDC Order** and trial/MSC orders.
2. Complete **statutory meet-and-confer**; prepare Local R. **560**-compliant declaration.
3. If dept/complex rules require IDC → schedule IDC; calendar any stay of motion deadlines.
4. **Reserve** motion date (email reservation); file and pay within **5 court days** (Local R. **520** / **521**).
5. **eFile** via approved EFSP into **Odyssey eFileCA** (Local R. **1800–1860**); mandatory for represented General Civil parties (post–Sept. 2, 2025).
6. Observe **CCP discovery cutoffs / expert exchange** unless a TSC/CMO resets them — do not assume a San Bernardino local standing cutoff.
7. Check tentative ruling under Local R. **561** after 3:00 p.m. the court day before hearing.
