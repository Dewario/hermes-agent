## 2024-2026 Amendment and Limit Conclusions

- **2024:** Section 2023.050 was amended by SB 235 (Stats. 2023, ch. 284, § 3), effective January 1, 2024, increasing the specified mandatory sanction from $250 to $1,000.
- **2026:** AB 1521 (Stats. 2025, ch. 200) amended § 2016.040 (meet-and-confer declaration), § 2023.010 (discovery misuse), and §§ 2030.020, 2031.020, and 2033.020 (unlawful-detainer timing), effective January 1, 2026.
- No 2024-2026 amendment was identified in the official current text for the other specifically enumerated provisions above.
- The special-interrogatory limit remains **35** under § 2030.030. Official form interrogatories are additional and are not capped at 35 by that section.
- The RFA limit remains **35** for matters not concerning document genuineness under § 2033.030. Genuineness requests are excluded from that numerical cap, subject to protection against abuse.
- The principal no-timely-response deemed-admission procedure remains **§ 2033.280**. Section 2033.290 instead governs further responses, although subdivision (e) allows deemed admissions after violation of an order compelling further responses.
