# King County Superior Court local civil rules — discovery research

**Research status:** Official King County sources checked July 19, 2026. This is a source-bounded practice reference, not legal advice. Confirm the issued Case Scheduling Order (CSO), assigned judge's procedures, and any rule amendments before acting.

## 1. Current King County local rules and version

The Court's live Local Civil Rules (LCR), Local General Rules (LGR), and Local Civil Arbitration Rules (LCAR) pages are the controlling online sources reviewed. The relevant live pages show amendments effective **September 1, 2025** for LCR 4, LCR 7, LCR 37, and LCR 40. The Court's 2026 comment-period page states that proposed 2026 amendments will become effective **September 1, 2026** after adoption. Accordingly, on the July 19, 2026 research date, do not call the proposals current rules.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 4, 7, 37, 40; LGR 30; LCAR 2.1, 4.2.
* **Practice point:** Check the live rule page and any emergency rule before filing. The Court's general local-rules page identifies an emergency criminal-rule change, but the reviewed discovery/civil pages show the 2025 effective dates above.
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 2. Civil case scheduling and the CSO

### Issuance and form

Under **LCR 4(a)**, when an initial pleading opens a new covered civil case, the Clerk prepares and files the scheduling order called the **“Case Schedule.”** Under **LCR 40(a)(1)**, the Clerk issues a trial date, case schedule, and judicial assignment for civil cases except those identified in LCR 4(b). A CR 40(a) notice of trial is not filed in a civil case.

**LCR 4(e)(1)-(2)** supplies the standard CSO form. It is a court-established, case-type-customized schedule, set by General Order, and uses `F` (weeks after filing) and `T` (weeks before trial). The current standard form includes:

* confirmation of joinder and statement-of-arbitrability deadline: `F+23`;
* disclosure of possible primary witnesses: `T-22`;
* disclosure of possible additional witnesses: `T-16`;
* discovery cutoff: `T-8`;
* exchange of expected witness/exhibit lists and documentary exhibits: `T-3`;
* dispositive-pretrial-motion hearing deadline: `T-2`; and
* trial: `T`.

The `T-8` discovery entry matches **LCR 37(g)**, which requires completion of discovery 56 calendar days before trial. The CSO governed by the specific case controls the actual date; do not calculate a deadline from the form if the issued order differs.

### Changing the CSO

**LCR 4(d)** permits modification for good cause, except that a trial-date change follows LCR 40(e). A motion to amend must include a proposed amended CSO. Parties cannot generally amend a CSO by stipulation without the assigned judge's approval. Two limited stipulations may extend up to 14 days without prior judge approval: the primary/additional-witness disclosure deadlines and the discovery cutoff; each must include the specified no-continuance language.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 4(a), (d), (e); LCR 37(g); LCR 40(a), (e).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 3. Discovery-motion procedure

### Meet and confer / certificate

**LCR 37(e)** says “Conference of Counsel. See CR 26(i),” and **LCR 37(f)** says “Certificate of Compliance. See CR 26(i).” Thus, the applicable meet-and-confer and certification requirements are in Washington **CR 26(i)**, incorporated by the local rule rather than restated in LCR 37. For a motion for discovery exceeding the LCR 26 limits, **LCR 26(b)(5)(B)** expressly requires a CR 26(i) certificate with the motion.

### Motion format, timing, evidence, and declarations

Unless another rule supplies a different procedure, **LCR 7(b)** governs civil motions:

* Serve and file motion documents by 4:30 p.m. **nine judicial days** before the requested consideration date; opposing papers are due four judicial days before, and strict replies two judicial days before. **LCR 7(b)(4).**
* File a Notice of Court Date. **LCR 7(b)(5)(A).**
* Combine the motion and memorandum into one document. It must state requested relief, material facts, issues, evidence, and authority. **LCR 7(b)(5)(B)(i)-(v).**
* For evidence, deposition testimony, discovery pleadings, and documents relied upon must be quoted verbatim or relevant pages attached to a **declaration identifying the documents**. **LCR 7(b)(5)(B)(iv).**
* Initial and opposition memoranda are capped at **4,200 words** and replies at **1,750 words**, absent advance authorization; the signature block must certify the word count. **LCR 7(b)(5)(B)(vi).**
* Deliver working copies when served; electronic working copies may be submitted through the Clerk's eFiling application, subject to the listed exceptions. **LCR 7(b)(4)(F).**

Most motions are decided without oral argument. A party may request it by placing “ORAL ARGUMENT REQUESTED” prominently on the first page of the motion or opposition. **LCR 7(b)(3), (4)(C).**

### Motion to compel

No distinct King County local “motion to compel” form was located. The motion normally proceeds under **LCR 7**, with the underlying relief and sanctions supplied by the Washington Civil Rules (including CR 37) and the CR 26(i) conference/certificate requirement incorporated by **LCR 37(e)-(f)**. Discovery motions are ordinarily presented to the assigned judge. See LCR 40(b) below.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 7(b); LCR 26(b)(5)(B); LCR 37(e)-(f).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 4. Does the mandatory CSO set discovery and expert cutoffs by default?

**Yes for discovery and witness-disclosure dates; not as a distinct “expert cutoff.”**

The LCR 4(e) standard CSO includes:

* `T-22` primary-witness disclosure;
* `T-16` additional-witness disclosure; and
* `T-8` discovery cutoff.

Under **LCR 26(k)**, those witness disclosures expressly include persons with relevant factual **or expert** knowledge whom the party reserves the option to call. For experts, disclose the expert's opinions and basis and a brief description of qualifications. **LCR 37(g)** makes the general discovery completion deadline 56 calendar days before trial. The rules reviewed do not establish an independent local expert-disclosure or expert-discovery cutoff separate from the CSO's witness and discovery deadlines.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 4(e)(2); LCR 26(k); LCR 37(g).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 5. Expert witnesses, lists, and depositions

### Expert disclosures and lists

By the CSO's primary-witness disclosure date, each party must disclose all persons with relevant factual or expert knowledge whom it reserves the option to call. Additional witnesses whose relevance first appeared after primary disclosure are due by the additional-witness date. **LCR 26(k)(1)-(2).**

For an expert, the disclosure must include the person's name, address, and phone number plus: (1) a summary of opinions, (2) the basis for those opinions, and (3) a brief description of qualifications. **LCR 26(k)(3).** Failure can lead to sanctions, including witness exclusion, under **LCR 26(k)(4)**.

Separately, parties exchange the witnesses they **expect** to call, exhibit lists, and documentary exhibits no later than 21 days before trial. **LCR 4(j).** This is not a substitute for timely discovery responses identifying experts; LCR 4's official comment says disclosure of known fact or expert witnesses should not be delayed to CSO deadlines.

### Expert depositions and cutoff

**LCR 26(b)(3)** generally limits a party to ten depositions, one day of seven hours each, with one deposition allowed for two seven-hour days. The rule does not create a separate expert-deposition category or deadline. Expert depositions must therefore fit within the general **LCR 37(g)** cutoff (56 calendar days before trial) unless the court orders otherwise or a valid stipulation applies. Additional discovery beyond the limits may be stipulated to or requested by motion under **LCR 26(b)(5)**.

Because deposition logistics, CR 30/26 expert-discovery requirements, and any judge-specific scheduling order can affect practice, confirm this application to a particular matter.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 4(j), (e); LCR 26(b)(3), (5), (k); LCR 37(g).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `true`

## 6. Mandatory arbitration and discovery

King County's local arbitration rules are cited **LCAR** (not “MAR”); the statewide framework is in the Superior Court Civil Arbitration Rules (SCCAR) and RCW 7.06.

Under **LCAR 2.1(a)**, a claim filed after September 1, 2019 is subject to civil arbitration if it does not exceed **$100,000**, exclusive of attorney fees, interest, and costs. The Court's current arbitration page additionally states the eligibility criteria: a civil, non-domestic Superior Court action (not a limited-jurisdiction appeal), money damages only, and no claim above the $100,000 limit.

* A party seeking mandatory arbitration files the Court-prescribed Statement of Arbitrability before the CSO deadline. Late transfer requires an order and good cause. **LCAR 2.1(b).**
* An objection based on the objector's own claim/counterclaim must be served and filed within 14 days; absent a timely response, the statement is deemed correct. **LCAR 2.1(c).**
* On transfer, the **Case Schedule is stricken. LCAR 2.1(f).**
* Discovery directly authorized by SCCAR 4.2 proceeds under the civil rules, but additional discovery is evaluated by the arbitrator, who balances benefits, burdens, and expenses. Discovery motions are determined by the arbitrator. Pending discovery is stayed on arbitrator assignment unless the arbitrator orders otherwise, the parties stipulate, or SCCAR 4.2 authorizes it. **LCAR 4.2.**

* **local_rule_id:** LCAR 2.1; LCAR 4.2.
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 7. Trial assignment and civil-motion departments

For covered civil cases, the Clerk assigns a judge and trial date at filing. **LCR 40(a)(1).** Under **LCR 40(b)**, civil motions and proceedings go to the assigned judge under LCR 7; if no judge is assigned, they go to the Ex Parte and Probate Department under LCR 40.1, unless one of LCR 40(b)'s listed exceptions applies.

That means a typical discovery motion, including a motion to compel or protective-order motion, is normally for the assigned judge. **LCR 26(c)** expressly says protective-order motions (even agreed ones) go to the assigned judge, not the Ex Parte Department; if no judge is assigned, they go to the Respective Chief Judge. The Court's April 2026 Civil Department Manual likewise lists discovery motions and protective-order motions as assigned-judge matters, ordinarily without oral argument.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 26(c); LCR 40(a), (b); LCR 40.1; LCR 7(b).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 8. E-filing and e-service

**LGR 30(b)(4)(A)** requires attorneys to e-file all documents through the Clerk's online eFiling application unless an exception or approved waiver applies. Non-attorneys may e-file but are not required to do so.

**LGR 30(b)(4)(B)** requires a party that e-files to e-serve through that application's e-service function; this service is effective under CR 5, subject to stated exceptions (including required personal service, an unrepresented non-registered recipient, or an attorney waiver). Attorneys must promptly register for e-service in every case in which they appear, absent waiver.

The Clerk identifies its application as the **KC Script Portal**. It supports e-filing, e-service registration and management, electronic working copies, and Ex Parte via the Clerk. For a motion, also follow the LCR 7 working-copy requirements; e-working copies may be submitted through the application where permitted.

* **local_rule_id:** LGR 30(b)(4); LCR 7(b)(4)(F).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`

## 9. Meet-and-confer or joint-stipulation prerequisite

King County does require the **CR 26(i)** conference/certificate through **LCR 37(e)-(f)** for discovery disputes. It does **not** appear, from the reviewed LCR 7, 26, and 37 text, to impose a general local requirement that the parties file a joint stipulation before a discovery motion.

Do not confuse that point with the separate CSO-amendment rules. Under **LCR 4(d)**, the parties may use written stipulations only for the limited no-more-than-14-day extensions of certain witness and discovery deadlines; other CSO changes need the assigned judge's approval. Under **LCR 26(b)(5)(A)**, the parties may also stipulate to change discovery limits. Those provisions do not replace the CR 26(i) conference/certificate for a disputed discovery motion.

* **local_rule_id:** LCR 4(d); LCR 26(b)(5); LCR 37(e)-(f).
* **needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** `false`
