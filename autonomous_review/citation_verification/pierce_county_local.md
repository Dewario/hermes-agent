## 2. Civil Case Scheduling — Discovery & Expert Cutoffs

Pierce uses a **mandatory Order Setting Case Schedule (Form A)** issued at filing for most civil non-family cases, combined with **track-based deadlines** in the local rules—not a separate standalone “discovery order” apart from the case schedule.

### Mechanism

| Element | Rule | Summary |
|---------|------|---------|
| Who gets a schedule at filing | **PCLR 3(c)** | Most civil non-family cases **not** listed in PCLR 3(a) or 3(b) receive **Order Setting Case Schedule (Form A)** at filing |
| Track assignment | **PCLR 3(h)** | Expedited, Standard, Complex, Dissolution, LUPA, Collaborative Law tracks |
| Deadline table | **PCLR 3(i)** | All cutoff dates measured in **weeks from filing** (or schedule assignment) |
| Amendment | **PCLR 3(e)** | Court may modify dates/track on motion or sua sponte for good cause; **trial date** changes governed by **PCLR 40(g)** |
| Enforcement | **PCLR 3(l)** | Sanctions/terms for failure to comply with case schedule |

### Track-based discovery cutoff & trial (from filing)

| Track | Discovery cutoff | Trial | Interrogatory cap | Deposition notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Expedited** | 20 weeks | 26 weeks | 25 (subparts count separately) | Non-party depositions require **court permission** |
| **Standard** | 45 weeks | 52 weeks | 35 (subparts count separately) | No local deposition cap (CR limits apply) |
| **Complex** | 67 weeks | 78 weeks | 35 (subparts count separately) | No local deposition cap |
| **Dissolution** | 30 weeks | 36 weeks | 100 (subparts count separately) | No local deposition cap (family track) |

- **Requests for admission:** No numerical limit on any track (**PCLR 3(h)**).
- **Written discovery timing:** Must be propounded early enough to complete by cutoff (**PCLR 3(h)**).

### Scheduled events tied to witnesses/experts (**PCLR 3(i)** table)

Weeks from filing (Standard track shown; see rule table for all tracks):

| Event | Expedited | Standard | Complex |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Plaintiff primary witness disclosure | 12 | 25 | 38 |
| Defendant primary witness disclosure | 15 | 29 | 42 |
| Rebuttal witness disclosure | 17 | 36 | 57 |
| **Discovery cutoff** | **20** | **45** | **67** |
| Witness/exhibit lists & documentary exhibits | 21 | 47 | 70 |

**Case scheduling order vs. local rule:** The **Form A order** implements the **PCLR 3(h)–(i)** intervals. The local rule is authoritative for track definitions and default intervals; individual orders should match Form A / Appendix.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** false for rule text; **true** for confirming the **actual Form A dates** on a live case (always read the issued order).

## 3. Discovery Motion Procedure

### Where heard

- **All discovery motions** → **assigned judicial department** (**PCLR 7(a)(1)**).
- **Court Commissioners do not hear discovery motions** (**PCLR 7(b)(1)(B)**).
- Default motion calendar: **Friday mornings, 9:00 a.m.** (**PCLR 7(a)(1)**).

### Scheduling & briefing

| Requirement | Rule | Detail |
|-------------|------|--------|
| Note for Motion Docket | **PCLR 7(a)(3)(A)** | Required approved form; filed **with** motion and supporting papers |
| Filing/service deadline | **PCLR 7(a)(3)(A)** | No later than **close of business 7 court days** before hearing |
| Opposition | **PCLR 7(a)(5)** | By **12:00 noon 3 court days** before hearing |
| Reply | **PCLR 7(a)(6)** | By **12:00 noon 2 court days** before hearing |
| Working copies | **PCLR 7(a)(7)** | To assigned department or Court Administrator; hearing date & judge on first page; **fax/email not acceptable** |
| Confirmation | **PCLR 7(a)(9)** | Moving party confirms **5–3 court days** before hearing (LINX electronic confirmation available) |
| Proof of service | **PCLR 7(a)(4)** | Required by confirmation deadline; e-service alone does not satisfy filing proof (**PCLGR 30** cross-ref) |

### Page limits (general civil motions, including discovery)

| Document | Limit | Rule |
|----------|-------|------|
| Initial motion & opposition memoranda | **12 pages** | **PCLR 7(a)(8)** |
| Reply memoranda | **5 pages** | **PCLR 7(a)(8)** |
| MSJ (if relevant) | 24 / 12 pages | **PCLR 7(a)(8)**; longer MSJ briefing & 28-day notice: **PCLR 7(a)(11)** |

Exceeding limits requires **prior court authorization** (**PCLR 7(a)(8)**).

### State CR overlay (compel / protective orders)

Because **PCLR 27–37 are Reserved**, discovery-dispute substance is governed by **Washington CR 26–37**:

- **CR 26(i):** Counsel must **confer** before motions/objections under CR 26–37; compel/protective motions must include **certification that conferral requirements were met**.
- **CR 37(a):** Motion to compel requires **showing of compliance with CR 26(i)**.

Pierce does **not** duplicate these in a local LCR 37 equivalent (contrast King County **LCR 37(e)–(f)**, which expressly incorporate CR 26(i)).

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** false for Pierce local mechanics; confirm current **CR 26(i)** text on Washington Courts if state rules amended after this research.

## 4. Expert Witness Disclosure Rules & Cutoffs

### Local disclosure rule: **PCLR 26**

Applies to all cases with an Order Setting Case Schedule (**PCLR 26(a)**).

| Disclosure | Deadline | Content |
|------------|----------|---------|
| **Primary witnesses** (lay & expert) | Date on case schedule | All persons party may call at trial (**PCLR 26(b)**) |
| **Rebuttal witnesses** | Date on case schedule | Witnesses whose relevance arose after primary disclosures (**PCLR 26(c)**) |
| **Expert content** | — | Summary of anticipated opinions & basis; qualifications or CV (**PCLR 26(d)(3)**) |
| **Treating physicians** | — | Treated as **expert and fact** witnesses (**PCLR 26(d)(3)**) |
| **Supplementation** | — | Does not replace CR duty to supplement discovery (**PCLR 26(e)**) |
| **Interrogatory limits** | Track-based | Cross-ref **PCLR 3(h)** / **PCLR 26(f)** |

### Cutoff dates (Standard track example — **PCLR 3(i)**)

- Plaintiff primary disclosure (includes experts): **week 25**
- Defendant primary disclosure: **week 29**
- Rebuttal disclosure: **week 36**
- **Discovery cutoff: week 45**
- Witness/exhibit exchange: **week 47**

Expert **depositions** and CR 26(b) expert discovery remain subject to the **discovery cutoff** and CR limits; Pierce does not add a separate local expert-deposition rule beyond PCLR 26 and the schedule.

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** false for rule numbers; confirm **Form A** dates on the case.

## 5. Mandatory Arbitration (MAR)

Pierce implements RCW 7.06 through **PCLSCCAR** (local overlay on statewide SCCAR).

### Threshold — **confirmed $100,000 (not $50,000)**

| Provision | Rule | Text |
|-----------|------|------|
| Purpose / cap | **PCLSCCAR 1.1(a)** | Arbitration for disputes involving claims of **$100,000.00 or less** |
| Net claim definition | **PCLSCCAR 1.2** | “Claim” = **net value** after comparative negligence/set-offs (e.g., $140k damages × 50% fault = $70k claim) |

**Historical note:** Washington legislation raised the statewide mandatory arbitration ceiling; Pierce local rules and comments reflect the **$100,000** cap (prior era commonly cited **$50,000**—King County **LCAR 2.1(a)** documents claims filed **before Sept. 1, 2019** at $50k and **after** at $100k). Pierce **PCLSCCAR 1.2** (amended effective Sept. 1, 2020) uses the **$100,000** figure without a split-date provision in the current text.

### Discovery in arbitration

- Statement of Arbitrability: **PCLSCCAR 2.1** — generally by **discovery cutoff**; after cutoff only by leave.
- Restricted discovery under **PCLSCCAR 4.2** once case transfers to arbitration.
- Arbitration discovery motions go to the **arbitrator**, not the superior court judge (**PCLSCCAR 4.2(a)**).

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** false for current $100k threshold in PCLSCCAR; confirm whether **net claim** calculation matches damages theory on your case.

## 6. Trial Assignment / Department Assignments for Civil Motions

### Case assignment — **PCLR 40**

| Topic | Rule | Practice |
|-------|------|----------|
| Assignment at filing | **PCLR 40(b)** | Case assigned to a **judicial department** at filing; remains there unless reassigned |
| Pretrial motions | **PCLR 40(b)** | Assigned department hears pretrial motions noted to its calendar |
| Trial date in scheduled cases | **PCLR 40(c)** | Trial date appears on **Order Setting Case Schedule**; change per **PCLR 40(g)** |
| Presiding judge / reassignment | **PCLR 40(a), 40(e)** | Court Administrator under Presiding Judge |

### Civil motion calendar — **PCLR 7(a)(1)**

- **Discovery motions:** assigned department only (not commissioners, not ex parte except agreed venue orders).
- **Friday 9:00 a.m.** motion docket unless specially set.
- Judicial departments listed in **PCLR 0.2(a)** (Departments 1–10+ as of 2025 rules).

## 8. Joint Stipulation or Meet-and-Confer Before Discovery Motions

### Meet-and-confer / certification

| Requirement | Authority | Pierce-specific? |
|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| Good-faith conferral before CR 26–37 motions | **CR 26(i)** | **No** — applies statewide |
| Certification in compel/protective motion | **CR 26(i)** | **No** local duplicate |
| Showing on motion to compel | **CR 37(a)** | Compliance with **CR 26(i)** required |
| Declaration format | **GR 30** | Unsworn declaration permitted where affidavit allowed |

**Pierce does not** appear to require a **joint stipulation** before filing discovery motions. **PCLR 27–37 are Reserved.**

The conferral obligation for the **Confirmation of Joinder** form (**PCLR 19(d)**) applies to **joinder reporting**, not discovery motions.

### Practical Pierce requirements beyond CR 26(i)

- **Note for Motion Docket** + **confirmation** (**PCLR 7(a)(3)(A), 7(a)(9)**)
- **Working copies** to assigned judge (**PCLR 7(a)(7)**)
- **Page limits** on memoranda (**PCLR 7(a)(8)**)

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** false that no Pierce local rule adds to CR 26(i); always include **CR 26(i) certification** (typically in motion body or counsel declaration) on compel/protective motions.

## 9. Pierce vs. King County — Plaintiff Attorney Quick Differences

Comparison uses **King County LCR effective September 1, 2024** (https://cdn.kingcounty.gov/-/media/king-county/depts/dja/local-rules/2024-kc-superior-court-local-rules.pdf). Confirm King’s current edition before relying on King-specific deadlines.

| Issue | Pierce County | King County |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **E-filing portal** | **LINX** (**PCLGR 30**) | **File & Serve / KCSC e-filing** (**LGR 30**) — not LINX |
| **Odyssey case search** | Excluded from Odyssey Portal | Excluded from Odyssey Portal |
| **Local CR 37 rule** | **Reserved** (PCLR 27–37) | **LCR 37** — discovery completion cutoff, CR 26(i) cross-refs |
| **Discovery cutoff extension by stipulation** | **No 14-day stipulation rule found**; amend schedule via **PCLR 3(e)** (court order) | **LCR 4(d)(2)** — parties may extend cutoff up to **14 days** by written stipulation (with anti-continuance language) |
| **Interrogatory limits** | **25** (expedited) / **35** (standard/complex); **subparts = separate** (**PCLR 3(h)**) | **40** total incl. discrete subparts, or **15** additional if pattern ROgs used (**LCR 26(b)(2)**) |
| **Deposition limits** | Standard/complex: **no local cap**; expedited: non-party deps need permission | **10 depositions**, 7 hours/day; one 2-day dep (**LCR 26(b)(3)**) |
| **RFA limits** | **No limit** (**PCLR 3(h)**) | **25** RFAs (+ auth docs) (**LCR 26(b)(4)**) |
| **Motion briefing metric** | **Page** limits: 12 / 5 (**PCLR 7(a)(8)**) | **Word** limits: 4,200 / 1,750 (**LCR 7(b)(5)(B)(vi)**) |
| **Motion lead time** | **7 court days** before hearing (**PCLR 7(a)(3)(A)**) | **9 judicial days** before consideration (**LCR 7(b)(4)(A)**) |
| **Motion confirmation** | **Required** (**PCLR 7(a)(9)**) | **Not required** (**LCR 7(b)(4)(I)**) |
| **Discovery motions forum** | Assigned **judge** only (**PCLR 7(a)(1)**) | Assigned **judge** (**LCR 40(b)**) |
| **Commissioners & discovery** | **Do not** hear discovery motions | Commissioners handle many non-discovery matters; discovery to assigned judge |
| **Expert disclosure content** | Opinions, basis, qualifications/CV; treating MD = expert (**PCLR 26(d)(3)**) | Similar (**LCR 26(k)(3)(C)**) |
| **MAR threshold (current)** | **$100,000** net claim (**PCLSCCAR 1.1–1.2**) | **$100,000** (post–Sept. 1, 2019 filings) (**LCAR 2.1(a)**) |
| **Trial readiness** | Pretrial/ADR under **PCLR 16**; joint statement of evidence **PCLR 16(b)(4)** | **Joint Confirmation of Trial Readiness** (**LCR 16(a)(1)**) |
| **Case schedule amendment by stipulation** | Generally requires **court** approval (**PCLR 3(e)**) | Limited **14-day** stipulation extensions for witness disclosure & discovery cutoff (**LCR 4(d)**) |

**needs_attorney_rule_confirm:** **true** for King County 2025–2026 local rule updates and for any Pierce **General Order** or **Form A** revision not captured in the Sept. 1, 2025 PDF.

## 10. Plaintiff-Side Discovery Checklist (Pierce Superior Court)

1. **On filing:** Receive and serve **Order Setting Case Schedule (Form A)** (**PCLR 3(c)**); identify **track** (**PCLR 3(h)**).
2. **Calendar** from **PCLR 3(i):** witness disclosures, discovery cutoff, dispositive-motion cutoff, trial.
3. **Propound discovery** early enough to finish by cutoff; respect **interrogatory caps** (**PCLR 3(h)**).
4. **Disclose experts** (incl. treating physicians) by primary/rebuttal deadlines (**PCLR 26**).
5. **Before discovery motion:** comply with **CR 26(i)** conferral + certification; file **Note for Motion Docket** ≥7 court days out (**PCLR 7**).
6. **Confirm** motion on LINX 5–3 court days before hearing (**PCLR 7(a)(9)**).
7. **Deliver working copies** to assigned department (**PCLR 7(a)(7)**).
8. **E-file/e-serve** via **LINX** (**PCLGR 30**).
9. **Evaluate MAR:** if net claim ≤ **$100,000**, anticipate **PCLSCCAR** transfer and reduced discovery.
