## Findings

- **No drift.** The eFiling effective date (Sept 2 2025), the Odyssey eFileCA EFM, the 1810.B operative rule, the 1800-1860 eFiling block, and CRC 3.20 preemption all confirm against official sources.
- **CRC 3.20 is load-bearing for the pack design:** it is the reason the San Bernardino pack carries case-management/local-motion rules (400-418, 520-571) but no local discovery substance — discovery substance is preempted and lives in CCP/CRC. The pack correctly reflects this.
- **Hermes vs Codex rule sets are complementary, not conflicting:** both fall in the same confirmed ranges (400-418 Delay Reduction; 520-571 civil law and motion). The reconciliation's "Codex has some San Bernardino case-management rules Hermes lacks" is accurate and the merge should union them.
- **Limitation:** the full PDF body was not byte-fetched (WebFetch does not render the PDF); verdicts rest on the official index/headings + the court's own explanatory pages. A byte-level confirm of 403/411.1/411.2/412/415/416/418 text would require downloading the PDF. The existence and range are confirmed; the exact subsection text is not.

## Cross-claim implications (Axis 2)

- No Axis-2 cross-claim is solely about San Bernardino, but the CRC 3.20 preemption finding supports the broader claim that local packs carry case-management rules only, not discovery substance.
