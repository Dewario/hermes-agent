## Findings

- **No drift.** All seven real case cites confirm against court-published opinions and reliable secondary sources. Both common-error cites (53 Cal.4th 1210 for Sargon; 1 Cal.5th 865 for Sanchez) are confirmed wrong. Both fictitious cites ("State v. Frye (1995)", "Copeland-Bryant (2018)") are confirmed nonexistent.
- **The CA Supreme Court citing its own Sanchez opinion in a 2022 case** is the strongest possible confirmation that "People v. Sanchez (2016) 63 Cal.4th 665" is the correct cite.
- **FRE 702 2023 amendment is a real, current nuance** the skill should continue to track: federal expert admissibility now requires the court to find it is "more likely than not" that the testimony meets the admissibility requirements (eff. Dec 1 2023). The skill's note that "WA ER 702 does NOT track the 2023 FRE 702 amendment" is correct and is a meaningful plaintiff-side distinction (federal-court experts face a stricter gate than WA-state experts on the same methodology).
- **Sargon as sword + shield:** confirmed Sargon is both a shield (keep plaintiff's experts in) and a sword (exclude defense speculative alternative-cause opinions) — this pairs directly with the § 801.1 medical-causation symmetry lever from Cluster 1. The skill's pairing of Sargon + § 801.1 for plaintiff-side damages work is well-founded.
