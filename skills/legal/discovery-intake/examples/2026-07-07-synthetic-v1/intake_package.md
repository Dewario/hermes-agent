## Incident Summary

**Pre-Incident Context**

J.T. was working as a freight conductor on the Extra Board, North District, for Test Valley Railroad. On the evening of November 12, 2024, J.T. was assigned to a pick-up operation on Main Line Track 2 at Northgate Yard (Milepost 47.3). The locomotive was TVRR #4721, a GP38-2, with a consist of 47 mixed freight cars totaling 4,812 trailing tons. The crew consisted of J.T. (conductor), L.M. (engineer), B.C. (switchman), and S.N. (conductor trainee) — a crew of four.

Weather conditions were clear with a temperature of approximately 38°F. It was dark, and J.T. described the yard lighting on Track 2 as "dim" — a condition J.T. states had been reported as insufficient "several times" previously but never remedied. The crew size at Northgate had been reduced from five to four persons approximately six months before the incident (May 2024). J.T. states that employees had complained about the reduced crew size to the union representative. (Note: the intake-facts file describes the reduction as from four to three; the questionnaire describes it as from five to four. This discrepancy is flagged in the Missing-Information List and requires attorney clarification.)

**Incident Mechanics**

At approximately 22:30 hours, the crew was making a pick-up on Track 2. A yard crew had previously left a cut of cars on the track. According to J.T., the standing cars were not properly secured — no handbrakes had been set. When locomotive TVRR #4721 coupled to the cut to make the joint, the standing cars rolled instead of coupling. J.T. was on the ground lining a switch when the unexpected movement occurred. J.T. attempted to get clear but was struck on the left shoulder by the steps of an adjacent car, throwing J.T. into the switch stand. J.T. landed hard on the left side, impacting the shoulder, ribs, and hip.

**Post-Incident Response**

Engineer L.M. called in the incident via radio. J.T. was transported by ambulance to County General Hospital, arriving at approximately 23:15 hours. Initial treatment included x-rays and CT imaging. Rib fractures (left ribs 5–7) were identified on ER x-ray, and a concussion with post-concussive symptoms was noted in the ER record. J.T. was discharged on November 13, 2024.

J.T. has not returned to work since the incident. A recorded statement was taken by a TVRR claims agent by telephone on November 14, 2024.

## Pitfalls

1. **Crew-size discrepancy:** The intake facts file and the questionnaire give conflicting accounts of the crew-size reduction (4→3 versus 5→4). This is flagged in the Missing-Information List. Do NOT adopt either version without attorney clarification. Both are recorded in the intake for resolution.

2. **Recorded statement to claims agent:** J.T. gave a recorded statement to a TVRR claims agent on November 14, 2024 — two days after the incident. This is a significant fact. The recording must be obtained in discovery, and the attorney should advise J.T. regarding any further communications with TVRR representatives.

3. **Prior close-call documentation:** The August 2024 close-call at the same location is central to the notice theory. J.T. says it was reported to supervisor R.K., but the content of the report and TVRR's response are unknown. Whether the close call was formally documented or only verbally reported affects its evidentiary weight.

4. **Do not assume client facts are complete.** The Missing-Information List contains 45 items and is itself a primary product of this intake. Gaps should be pursued, not papered over.

5. **Do not draft discovery directed to the client.** Discovery (Section 12) targets TVRR and third parties. Client interview follow-up questions (Section 10) are the separate, appropriate mechanism for obtaining information from J.T. Do not confuse these.

6. **Banned language:** Do not use "proves," "establishes," or "demonstrates" in any section. Use "evidence supports," "evidence suggests," or "requires attorney review" / "requires investigation."

7. **FELA causation is featherweight.** The defendant's negligence need only play any part, even the slightest, in causing the injury. This is not general tort causation. Apply this standard when evaluating the strength of liability theories.

8. **Assumption of risk is NOT a defense in FELA** (45 U.S.C. § 54). Do not assess or imply that J.T. assumed the risk of working on the ground.

9. **Contributory negligence reduces damages but does not bar recovery** (45 U.S.C. § 53). Even if TVRR alleged contributory fault, it would only affect damages, not liability.

10. **Safety Appliance Act and Locomotive Inspection Act claims are strict liability.** No proof of negligence is required for FSAA (handbrake) or LIA (locomotive equipment) theories. These are independent bases for recovery.

11. **SOL Issue Flag only — never compute a limitations date.** This intake records the need for limitations analysis but does not calculate or assert a deadline. The calculation is a legal determination for the attorney.

12. **RRTA tax treatment applies.** FELA lost-earnings projections must use net-after-RRTA methodology (Tier I and Tier II), not FICA. Economic experts must be instructed accordingly.

13. **Time-sensitive evidence.** Surveillance video, event recorder data, and audio recordings may be subject to routine overwrite or short retention policies. Hold letters should issue immediately upon attorney authorization.

14. **All output is draft for attorney review.** No section of this intake package constitutes a legal conclusion. Every analysis section requires attorney verification against the governing authority.
