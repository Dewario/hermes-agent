# Case Facts — SYN-SMOKE-EXPERT (SYNTHETIC / NON-CLIENT / TEST ONLY)

## Incident

On April 12, 2026, at approximately 3:10 a.m., a Union Pacific freight train
collided with a pickup truck at a rural grade crossing in King County,
Washington. The crossing was marked by a crossbuck sign but had no active
warning devices (no gates, lights, or bells). The locomotive horn was sounded
approximately 2 seconds before impact — well short of the required distance.

The driver, a 42-year-old railroad employee commuting home after a long shift,
suffered a traumatic brain injury (TBI) and multiple fractures. The vehicle
was destroyed.

## Regulatory backdrop

The Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) Hours of Service Act limits for the
train crew were exceeded by approximately 90 minutes at the time of the
collision. The railroad's own operating rules required horn-sounding at least
15 seconds before a public crossing.

## Damages picture

The plaintiff was hospitalized for 18 days, required neurosurgery, and remains
in inpatient rehabilitation. He has not returned to work as a locomotive
engineer. Pre-injury earnings averaged $112,000/year. Prognosis includes
permanent cognitive deficits and likely future medical needs including
ongoing therapy, medication management, and possible retraining.

The collision caused substantial **wage loss** and diminished future earning
capacity. **Lost earnings** are expected to continue indefinitely given the
permanent cognitive deficits. A life care plan will be needed to project
**future medical costs** and care needs. The plaintiff's **earning capacity**
is permanently impaired, and vocational retraining feasibility is uncertain.
