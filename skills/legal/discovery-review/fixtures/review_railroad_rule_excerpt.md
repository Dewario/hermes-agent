## TEST VALLEY RAILROAD — OPERATING RULES (SELECTED EXCERPTS)

### GCOR Rule 5.8 — Securement of Equipment Left Unattended

5.8.1 — When equipment is left unattended, it must be secured to prevent unintentional movement. At a minimum:
- A sufficient number of handbrakes must be applied to hold the equipment.
- The number of handbrakes required is determined by the grade, number of cars, weight of the equipment, and weather conditions.
- When air brakes are used for securement, the air pressure must be monitored, and the equipment must not be left unattended for more than 4 hours on air.

5.8.2 — Before leaving equipment, the employee responsible for securement must verify that the equipment will not move. This includes testing the handbrakes by attempting to move the equipment after securement.

5.8.3 — Any employee who observes equipment that has not been properly secured must immediately report the condition and take action to prevent movement if it can be done safely.

### GCOR Rule 6.3 — Switching Operations (Selected)

6.3.4 — Before coupling to standing equipment, the crew must verify that the equipment is properly secured and will not roll upon coupling.

6.3.5 — When operating on a grade, the locomotive must be stopped before coupling to standing equipment. Coupling while in motion on a grade is prohibited.

### TVRR Standard YP-7 — Yard Lighting Requirements

Section 3.2: All yard tracks used for switching operations during hours of darkness must be illuminated to a minimum level of 5 foot-candles at ground level.

Section 3.4: Monthly lighting inspections shall be conducted, and any fixture below the 5 foot-candle minimum shall be repaired or replaced within 14 calendar days.

### TVRR Safety Rule SR-14 — Crew Size Requirements

Section 2.1: Minimum crew size for switching operations is 4 persons: engineer, conductor, and two ground personnel (switchman/brakeman).

Note: This rule was revised effective May 1, 2024, reducing the minimum crew size from 5 to 4 persons for yard switching operations. The prior version (effective January 1, 2020) required a minimum of 5.

### FRA Regulations (49 CFR Part 218 — Railroad Operating Practices)

Section 218.99 — Each railroad shall adopt and comply with an operating rules program. Each railroad shall periodically instruct each affected employee on the meaning and application of its operating rules.
