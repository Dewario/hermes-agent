## Pitfalls

This review package is an attorney work product draft prepared from synthetic test fixtures for pilot validation of the legal discovery review workflow. The following limitations apply and must be considered before any use in actual case preparation:

**Incomplete Production Set.** Only 7 of 15 items described in the production cover letter were available for review in this pilot. Eight additional items totaling approximately 78 pages — including J.T.'s personnel and training records, D.W.'s personnel file and statement, lighting inspection reports, locomotive inspection records, training bulletins, crew time slips, and safety meeting minutes — have not been reviewed. The analysis in Sections 4, 5, 6, and 12 may change materially once those documents are reviewed. The document inventory and gap analysis reflect this limitation and identify every un-reviewed item by Bates range.

**No Legal Opinions Rendered.** This document describes facts, identifies inconsistencies, and flags issues requiring attorney attention. It does not render legal conclusions regarding liability, negligence, causation, or damages. The phrase "evidence supports an inference" describes factual patterns; the application of legal standards — including FELA's standard for causation — is reserved for the attorney.

**Privilege Assessment Is Preliminary.** The privilege screen in Section 7 is a preliminary identification of documents bearing confidentiality markings or subject to privilege claims. Only an attorney can determine whether to challenge a privilege designation, seek in camera review, or move to compel production. The privilege log referenced in the cover letter has not been reviewed in this pilot.

**Damages Figures Are Preliminary.** All wage and medical-cost figures are extracted from the produced documents and have not been independently verified. No economic loss projection, present-value calculation, or lifetime earnings analysis has been performed. All damages figures require expert review by a qualified forensic economist and vocational expert before use in settlement negotiations, mediation, or trial. RRTA net-after-tax methodology must be applied by an accountant familiar with railroad retirement taxation.

**Medical Causation Requires Expert Development.** The treating physician's causation note provides a temporal association between the November 12, 2024 incident and J.T.'s injuries. Establishing legal causation to the applicable standard requires attorney evaluation of the medical evidence, potentially supplemented by independent medical examination and expert opinion. The MRI results ordered by Dr. A.P. are not yet available in the reviewed documents.

**Gap Analysis Is Source-Limited.** Production gaps identified in Section 6 are based on the cover letter's description of the production and what would ordinarily exist in a railroad's business records. Documents may exist but not be described in the cover letter; documents described as not located may subsequently be found. The analysis assumes the cover letter is accurate with respect to what was produced and what was withheld.

**Deposition Outlines Are Seeds Only.** The deposition outlines in Section 10 are starting points generated from the reviewed documents. They do not replace comprehensive deposition preparation, which requires review of the complete production, consultation with the client, and development of a case-specific examination strategy.

**Contradiction Analysis Requires Context.** The contradictions identified in Section 12 are based on facial inconsistencies between documents. Resolution may depend on additional context — the sequence in which documents were created, the authors' access to information at the time of writing, and facts not yet in the record. Deposition testimony may resolve or deepen apparent contradictions.

**Synthetic Data Only.** All names, dates, Bates numbers, dollar figures, and case details are synthetic and drawn from the test fixture set maintained in the legal discovery review skill directory. This document contains no real client information and must not be used for any actual legal matter.
